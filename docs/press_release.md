# Press Release

## RoomLight — Simplicity and Serenity in Hotel Lighting

Wickie announced today a new lighting system for hotels — RoomLight, an in-room wall panel that replaces traditional light switches with hotel-standard lighting scenes and built-in sleep and wake-up programs.

Hotels aim for a consistent brand experience, yet room lighting often drifts over time — different brightness levels, a different “feel” from room to room, and a confusing mix of switches that guests must guess their way through. On top of that, many guests struggle with harsh light at night and abrupt wakeups, especially when traveling across time zones.

RoomLight replaces scattered switches with in-room wall panels and a set of hotel-standard lighting scenes — Welcome, Relax, Work, and Night — plus an intuitive brightness slider. Hotels can design these scenes once and sync them across every room, keeping the experience aligned over time. Built-in Sleep Assist and Wake-Up Sunrise -programs help guests wind down smoothly and wake up gently, turning lighting into a consistent part of the stay — not a nightly frustration.

“Hotels shouldn’t have to choose between a beautiful lighting brand and operational simplicity. RoomLight lets you design your lighting once, sync it everywhere, and keep every room consistent — while giving guests a better night’s sleep and a smoother wake-up,” said Product Lead Teemu Koivukangas from Wickie.

RoomLight is a turnkey service. After the in-room panels have been installed and integrated with the room lighting, Wickie runs a kick-off session to introduce the system and help your team define your hotel’s lighting scenes and standards. Once your presets are created and synced across rooms, guests can use RoomLight through a simple, intuitive interface with just a few buttons and a brightness slider.

“After we rolled out RoomLight, every room finally felt like the same hotel. The lighting is consistent, the panel is effortless to use, and guests keep mentioning the Sleep Assist and Wake-Up Sunrise features in reviews. It’s a small detail that makes the whole stay feel premium,” said Guest Experience and Quality Manager Heini Paijari from Kotoinen Marsu Hotels.

# FAQ

## Is RoomLight difficult for quests to use?

No — quests have only a few clear scene buttons to choose from and a simple brightness slider. No more guessing which switch does what.

## What happens if the network is down — do the lights still work?

Yes — core lighting control remains available in-room. The panel continues to operate the key scenes and brightness locally. Connectivity is mainly used for syncing standards and managing updates across rooms.

## How does RoomLight impact guest satisfaction and reviews?

Hotels typically see fewer complaints about confusing switches and harsh night lighting, and more positive mentions of comfort features like Sleep Assist and Wake-Up Sunrise.
